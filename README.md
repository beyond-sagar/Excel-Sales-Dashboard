📊 Sales Dashboard in Microsoft Excel

## Overview

This project is an interactive **Sales Dashboard** built entirely in **Microsoft Excel** to analyze retail sales performance from **2014–2017**. The dashboard transforms raw transactional data into meaningful business insights using Pivot Tables, Pivot Charts, Slicers, and Excel formulas.

The objective of this project was to practice data analysis, dashboard design, and business reporting while developing practical Excel skills that are widely used in analytics and business intelligence roles.


## Project Objectives

* Convert raw sales data into an interactive business dashboard.
* Track key performance indicators (KPIs) such as Revenue, Profit, Profit Margin, and Year-over-Year (YoY) Growth.
* Enable users to explore sales performance across multiple dimensions.
* Present business insights through clear and visually appealing charts.


## Dashboard Features

The dashboard provides:

* 📈 Total Revenue
* 💰 Total Profit
* 📊 Profit Margin
* 📅 Year-over-Year (YoY) Growth
* 🌍 Revenue by Region
* 🗂 Revenue by Category
* 📦 Revenue by Sub-Category
* 👥 Revenue by Customer
* 📆 Revenue by Year
* 📅 Revenue by Quarter
* 📌 Revenue by Month

Interactive filters allow users to quickly analyze different segments of the business.


## Key Business Insights

**1. Margin Concentration Risk**
Technology led revenue ($836K) but a single sub-category, Copiers, 
drove the highest profit ($55,617). Broad revenue masking narrow margin 
sources is a measurable business risk.

**2. Segment Profitability Paradox**
Consumer generated 50% of revenue but had the thinnest margin profile. 
Home Office which is smallest by revenue, delivered disproportionately high 
profit. Revenue share and profit share tell completely different stories.

**3. Seasonality Disguised as Growth**
Revenue grew 20.36% YoY (2016–17). But monthly trends reveal consistent 
Q4 spikes across all 4 years. Structural seasonality, not pure demand 
expansion, is driving the headline growth number.

**4. Regional Profit Efficiency Gap**
West led both revenue ($725K) and profit ($108K). 
But South, despite ranking last in revenue ($391K),
outpaced Central on profit($46K vs $39K).
High revenue does not guarantee strong margin,
Central is the clearest evidence of that.

**5. Office Supplies — Volume Without Value**
Office Supplies drove 60% of total order volume (22,906 units) but ranked 
third in revenue. High transaction frequency with low ticket value signals 
commodity-like demand with limited pricing power.

**6. Q4 Dependency Risk**
Q4 outperformed every other quarter across all 4 years consistently. 
A business generating a disproportionate share of annual revenue in one 
quarter carries inventory, staffing, and cash flow risks the annual 
figure alone would never reveal.

## How to Use

1. Download the workbook.
2. Open the `.xlsm` file in Microsoft Excel.
3. Enable content/macros if prompted.
4. Navigate to the **Dashboard** worksheet.
5. Use the available filters and slicers to interact with the dashboard.
